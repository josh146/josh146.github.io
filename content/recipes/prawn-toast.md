Deep fried prawn toast with sesame seeds (originating from [Hong Kong Cantonese cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shrimp_toast)) is a staple of western Chinese restaurants in
Australia. In addition to the version here, you can also spread the prawn paste *between* two
pieces of bread, or even in-between gua bao or mantou.

## Ingredients

* 750ml cups vegetable oil
* 40g (about 1/3 cup) white sesame seeds
* 4 slices white sandwich bread
* Sweet chilli sauce (optional)

### Prawn filling

* 225g raw prawn meat (chopped)
* 1/4 tsp salt
* 1/4 tsp caster sugar
* 1/4 tsp white pepper
* 1 tsp sesame oil
* 1 tsp soy sauce
* 1 egg white
* 1 tsp cornflour
* 1 clove garlic (finely minced)
* 1 tsp ginger (finely minced)
* 2 tbsp spring onion (finely diced)
* 2 tbsp coriander leaves (optional)

## Steps

1. Add the prawn meat to a food processor (or a container that will fit an immersion blender)
alongside the sugar, salt, and pepper, and blitz briefly to combine (about 10 seconds). Add the
sesame oil, soy sauce, egg white, and cornflour, and blitz again for about 30 seconds to emulsify.

1. Add the mixture to a bowl, and mix in the garlic, ginger, spring onion, and coriander leaves
(if using).

1. Cut the crusts off the bread, and spread the prawn mixture edge-to-edge on each piece before
cutting diagonally into two triangles. With the sesame seeds spread on a plate, press each piece of
bread prawn-side-down into the seeds to get full coverage.

1. Heat the oil in a heavy cast iron pan to 190ºC[ref]While deep frying is traditional, you can also choose to shallow fry.[/ref]. When at temperature, fry the prawn toast 4 pieces
at a time face down for 2.5 minutes, before flipping and frying for another minute. Drain on a
paper-towel lined tray.

1. Serve sprinkled with additional spring onion and with sweet chilli sauce for dipping.
