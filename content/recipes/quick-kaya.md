Kaya is a delicious, caramel-y coconut jam (albeit more like a custard than a jam) commonly found in
Singapore and Malaysia[ref]There are actually two common variants of kaya --- nyonya kaya, which is light green in colour and pandan flavoured, and Hainanese kaya, which is mixed with caramel and dark brown.[/ref].

The standard way to serve kaya is as part of a kaya toast set; spread between thin pieces of
Hainanese toast (sans crust), with two thick slabs of cold butter. Typically it is served with two
(very!) soft boiled eggs[ref]To make the soft boiled eggs, bring a saucepan of water to the boil,
remove from the heat, and add two eggs. Cover the pan with a lid, and leave for 6-7 minutes.
[/ref], served in a small bowl with dark soy sauce and white pepper[ref]Whenever I am in Singapore, Ya Kun is my go-to place for kaya toast.[/ref].

This recipe is based on the [quick coconut jam recipe](https://food52.com/recipes/90190-coconut-jam-kaya) by Jun, itself based on a [What To Cook Today
recipe](https://whattocooktoday.com/coconut-and-eggs-jam-srikaya-jam.html), which aims to reduce
the water content in the initial ingredients to reduce the length of time needed to thicken the
kaya. The changes involve coconut cream (instead of milk), 'pre'-caramelized sugar in the form of brown sugar, and egg yolks rather than whole eggs.

## Ingredients

* 240g coconut cream
* 50g granulated sugar
* 50g dark brown or palm sugar
* 4 egg yolks
* 2 pandan leaves (knotted) or 1/2 tsp pandan essence
* 2g salt

## Steps

1. Place the coconut cream, granulated sugar, palm sugar, salt, and egg yolks in a small saucepan and stir
together until smooth. Knot the pandan leaves and add it to the saucepan.

1. Heat the saucepan over medium heat for 10 minutes, stirring continuously. The kaya will thicken
as it cooks, and you want it to get to the consistency of a thick jam or custard. As it cooks, be
careful not to let the kaya boil, turning the heat down to low if bubbles are forming.

1. When done, let the kaya cool to room temperature before transferring to store in a jar. The kaya
can last for up to 3 weeks in the fridge.
